Auto Fraud — Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers about auto fraud, car dealer fraud, and your rights as an Arizona consumer.

How much does an auto fraud attorney cost?
In most auto fraud cases, nothing out of pocket. We work on a no-win, no-fee basis, and the law often requires the dealership to pay your attorney's fees when your claim succeeds — so you can pursue your case with no up-front cost.
What is auto dealer fraud?
Auto dealer fraud is any deceptive or illegal practice a dealership uses to sell or finance a vehicle — including hiding prior accident or flood damage, odometer rollback, bait-and-switch tactics, yo-yo financing, packing hidden fees into your loan, or selling a salvaged car as clean.
What is yo-yo financing or spot delivery?
Yo-yo financing (spot delivery) is when a dealer lets you take the car home before financing is final, then calls you back days later claiming the loan "fell through" and demanding a higher payment, bigger down payment, or higher interest rate. This is a common and often illegal auto fraud tactic.
Can I sue a car dealership for fraud?
Yes. If a dealer lied about a vehicle's condition, history, mileage, price, or financing, you may be able to sue for fraud and consumer protection violations — potentially recovering a refund, your money back, and additional damages. A free case review is the fastest way to know if you have a claim.
What is odometer fraud?
Odometer fraud (odometer rollback) is illegally altering or falsifying a vehicle's mileage to make it appear newer or less used than it really is. Federal law allows victims to recover up to three times their damages plus attorney's fees.
I bought a bad used car "as is" — do I still have a claim?
Possibly. An "as is" sticker does not protect a dealer who committed fraud or concealed material facts — like an undisclosed accident, salvage title, or rolled-back odometer. Used car fraud claims often survive "as is" language. Let us review your paperwork.
How long do I have to file an auto fraud claim in Arizona?
Time limits (statutes of limitation) apply and vary by claim type. It's important not to wait — contact our Arizona auto fraud attorneys as soon as possible so we can review the deadlines that apply to your case.
